SAT Grammar Cheat Sheet

Grammar
1. Period = Semicolon = Comma + and/but
2. 2 commas = 2 dashes = non-essential clause. When crossed out, the sentence still makes sense.
3. It’s = it is; Its = possessive form of it; its’ = does not exist
4. Colon = list or explanation. Need a complete sentence before but not after.
5. BEING, usually = WRONG
6. Could/would/should/might HAVE, not OF
7. No comma before or after a preposition (of, by, to, at, for, from, with, in, on) , or the word that
8. Comma before it, he, she, they usually = WRONG
9. Singular verbs end in –s, plural verbs do not end in –s (e.g. she reads, they read)
10. Keep pronouns consistent: one = one, you = you
11. Who is for people, which is for things, where is for places (e.g. the time/book where = WRONG)
12. Use who before a verb (who went, NOT whom went); use whom after a preposition (e.g. by whom, NOT by who).
13. All items in a list must match (noun, noun, noun or verb, verb, verb)
14. Always underline NOT, LEAST, and EXCEPT
